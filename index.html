Agreement types available

🤝

Mutual NDA

Used when both parties will be sharing confidential information with each other. Common for exploratory partnership discussions, joint ventures, or early-stage negotiations. Both parties agree to protect each other's information equally.

🔒

One-Way NDA

Used when only one party is sharing confidential information. For example, if you are pitching to a potential investor or showing a vendor your proprietary process, only your information needs protecting. The receiving party signs to protect it.

🏢

Partner Agreement

Formalizes a business collaboration. Covers scope of work, each party's responsibilities, compensation or revenue share, exclusivity, and duration. Use this when entering a new business relationship that goes beyond information sharing.

📦

Reseller Agreement

Authorizes a third party to resell your products or services. Sets out territory, pricing, exclusivity, and the specific products covered. Use this when onboarding a channel partner, distributor, or authorized reseller.

⚠️ Important reminders

  • All generated agreements are boilerplate templates. They are starting points, not final legal documents.
  • Have a qualified attorney review any agreement before signing, especially for high-value or complex arrangements.
  • Make sure the governing law and jurisdiction fields match where your company is incorporated or operates.
  • For the "Term" field, enter how long the agreement is active. For "Survival," enter how long confidentiality obligations continue after the agreement ends.
  • If a field is left blank, it will appear in the document with a placeholder. Make sure to fill those in before distributing.
